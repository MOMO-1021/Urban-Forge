## Project Overview

Our clients in Bellevue, Washington, requested a custom fireplace screen designed to provide safety and containment without obstructing the clean, open view of their dramatic linear gas fireplace. Located inside a double-height living room featuring a floor-to-ceiling concrete board chimney breast, the fireplace is a central visual focal point. 

The primary design challenge was to craft a screen that felt incredibly lightweight, thin, and minimalist, yet remained physically robust enough to resist warping or twisting under the intense thermal cycling of an active hearth. The solution was a flush-mounted, custom-woven blackened steel screen that integrates seamlessly into the fireplace opening, matching the clean lines of the home's contemporary architectural design.

---

## Heat Dissipation & Thermal Engineering

Fireplaces pose a unique challenge for metal fabricators due to **thermal expansion**. When metal is heated, it expands; when it cools, it contracts. If a metal frame is constructed too rigidly without built-in allowance for this expansion, or if it is fabricated from thin, sub-standard gauge metals, the intense heat will cause the frame to twist, buckle, or pull away from the masonry. 

To prevent this deformation, our team engineered the screen using a heavy **1/4" thick x 1-1/2" wide solid steel bar** for the primary outer frame. Solid bar stock has significantly greater thermal mass and rigidity than hollow tubing, allowing it to absorb heat without structural movement. We incorporated subtle, hidden expansion slots at the rear mounting tabs, allowing the frame to expand slightly along its width during active use without putting stress on the concrete board surround or causing structural binding.

---

## Mesh Selection & Flush-Weave Construction

While aesthetics were paramount, the screen's main functional duty is safety. We custom-ordered a high-gauge, high-tensile carbon steel woven mesh featuring a tight 1/8" opening pattern. This weave density is engineered to capture any flying embers or sparks while maintaining an exceptional 70% open-air visual transparency. 

To achieve the clean, "flush-mounted" look requested by the client, our fabricators milled a continuous 3/16" deep channel along the interior backside of the steel frame. The edges of the safety mesh were tucked neatly into this recessed channel and secured using a continuous, hidden backing bar that is plug-welded from behind. This advanced joinery technique completely conceals the cut edges of the mesh, resulting in a clean, smooth perimeter transition with no visible weld spatter, wire frays, or hardware clutter.

---

## Blackened Chemical Patinas & High-Heat Finishes

Standard powder coatings or liquid paints will fail, discolor, or emit toxic fumes when exposed directly to open flames or extreme heat. To ensure a safe and durable finish, we treated the steel using a multi-stage **chemical blackened patina**. 

In our workshop, the steel was acid-etched to clean the surface, followed by a hand-applied cold blackening compound that chemically alters the surface chemistry of the iron. This process turns the steel a deep, rich gunmetal-to-black color while allowing the natural grain and welding heat-marks of the raw steel to show through, adding tactile character. The screen was then baked to fully cure the chemical layers and sealed with a high-temperature siliconized wax rated to withstand temperatures up to **1200°F** without degrading or off-gassing.

---

## On-Site Installation & Fitting

Because the concrete-board wall was cast with precise, geometric lines, any gap between the fireplace frame and the wall would be highly visible. Our team conducted detailed laser measurements of the fireplace opening prior to fabrication. 

We engineered low-profile, weighted steel stabilizer feet that keep the screen perfectly vertical and flush against the concrete wall face. We integrated hand-forged, low-profile grab handles that are thermally isolated from the main frame, allowing the homeowners to safely lift and move the screen away from the hearth even when hot.
