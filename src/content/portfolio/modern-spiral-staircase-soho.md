## Project Overview

This striking modern spiral staircase serves as the central artery for a sprawling SoHo loft. The client, a prominent interior architect, challenged us to create a structure that felt both monolithic and weightless. 

## Fabrication Details

The central stringer was CNC precision-rolled in our shop to perfectly match the CAD designs provided by the architect. We utilized heavy-gauge hot-rolled steel to ensure minimal deflection (bounce) as you traverse the stairs. The treads themselves are thick slabs of white oak, creating a warm contrast against the industrial matte black powder coat finish.

## Architectural Integration

From a structural engineering standpoint, the staircase is entirely self-supporting at the base and the upper landing, eliminating the need for intermediary columns that would interrupt the open floor plan.

### Technical Specs
*   **Total Rise:** 144 inches
*   **Tread Count:** 18
*   **Finish:** Matte Black Architectural Powder Coat
*   **Tolerances:** +/- 1/16th of an inch across the entire structure.
