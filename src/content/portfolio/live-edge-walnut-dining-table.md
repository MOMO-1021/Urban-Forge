## Project Overview

This custom dining table was commissioned as a signature showpiece for a large, open-concept dining space in a modern lakefront home overlooking Lake Washington in Mercer Island, Washington. The primary architectural objective was to construct a monumental furniture piece that could comfortably seat twelve guests while visually bridging the organic warmth of live-edge wood with the bold, clean, and structural strength of industrial steel. By selecting a highly figured Claro Walnut slab and pairing it with custom-fabricated geometric steel pedestals, we created an heirloom-quality piece that perfectly anchors the home's contemporary aesthetic.

---

## Material Selection & Organic Warmth

Bespoke furniture relies heavily on sourcing the right raw substrates. For this commission, our timber specialist selected a rare, single-slab Claro Walnut log, air-dried for three years and kiln-finished to stabilize its internal moisture content at exactly 8%. This precise drying prevents future warping, twisting, or splitting in a temperature-controlled home environment. The slab is characterized by rich, flowing grain patterns, deep chocolate tones, and a striking live-edge perimeter that reflects the tree's natural silhouette. 

---

## Pedestal Base Engineering & Fabrication

To support a 12-foot hardwood slab weighing over 400 pounds, the base had to be exceptionally stable and rigid. We avoided standard, generic legs and opted to engineer custom geometric pedestal bases that mimic natural rock facets. 

In our workshop, we fabricated the pedestals out of heavy **3/4" thick hot-rolled carbon steel plates**. We drafted the complex, multi-angled geometries in 3D CAD to ensure perfect center-of-gravity balance and weight distribution. Each steel plate was precision cut using a high-definition CNC plasma table, and the mating edges were meticulously beveled at 45-degree angles to prepare for deep-penetration weld joints. 

Our fabricators welded the plates into hollow, three-dimensional faceted shapes using MIG welding with solid wire and an argon/CO2 shielding gas mixture. The corner welds were carefully ground down and hand-sanded with progressively finer grits to form perfectly crisp, seamless, and monolithic edges that appear to be folded from a single sheet of steel.

---

## Wood-to-Metal Joinery & Seasonal Expansion Engineering

Wood is a living material that continuously expands and contracts across its width in response to seasonal changes in relative humidity. Steel, conversely, is structurally rigid. Bolting a wide wood slab directly to a rigid steel frame without allowing for movement will inevitably cause the wood to split or crack over time. 

To overcome this, we engineered a custom slotted-bracket attachment system. We welded 1/2" steel mounting plates to the tops of the pedestals, incorporating elongated, oversized bolt slots. The wood slab is secured to these plates using structural lags with heavy brass washers. This allows the walnut slab to expand and contract naturally across its width while remaining completely flat and securely anchored to the steel base. Additionally, we designed custom **satin brass through-tenons** that lock the timber slab in place, adding a subtle, luxurious accent where wood and metal intersect.

---

## Finishing & Patina

To highlight the natural beauty of the steel base, we applied a custom chemical blackened patina rather than a standard flat paint. The patina reacts with the iron content, turning the surface a deep gunmetal color while allowing the natural mill scale and heat patterns from the welding process to remain subtly visible. The steel was then sealed with three coats of hand-rubbed architectural paste wax. The Claro Walnut slab was finished with a low-sheen, food-safe hardwax oil that penetrates deep into the wood fibers, preserving the tactile, organic texture of the timber.
